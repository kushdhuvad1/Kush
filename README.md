## About

I work at the intersection of **quantum cryptography**, **information theory**, and **network protocols** — with a focus on building systems that are secure even against quantum adversaries.

My M.Sc. thesis proposes a **Device-Independent QKD (DIQKD) protocol** grounded in computational hardness: combining Bell inequality (CHSH) violations with **trapdoor claw-free functions** constructed from the **Learning with Errors (LWE)** problem. The goal is security without trusting the quantum devices themselves.

Alongside theory, I build: I'm developing quantum network simulator modules (QKD, entanglement swapping, repeaters) under India's **National Quantum Mission** at IISER Bhopal.
