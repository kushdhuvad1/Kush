## About

I work at the intersection of **quantum cryptography**, **information theory**, and **network protocols** — focused on building systems that remain secure against quantum adversaries.

My M.Sc. thesis develops a **Device-Independent QKD (DIQKD) protocol** grounded in computational hardness. The core idea: combine CHSH Bell inequality violations with **trapdoor claw-free functions (TCFs)** constructed from the **Learning with Errors (LWE)** problem. This replaces the standard DIQKD requirement for strict space-like device separation with a computational assumption — enabling a classical client–quantum server architecture where neither device needs to be trusted. I have derived entropic key rate bounds under post-quantum assumptions and presented this work at **ISQIC-2025 (TCG CREST)**. The work is being prepared for publication.

Alongside theory, I build: I am a Project Associate at **IISER Bhopal** under India's **National Quantum Mission**, developing a quantum network simulator covering QKD protocols, entanglement swapping, and quantum repeater modules, with realistic noise and channel models.

I am also investigating **operational quantum reference frames** with Prof. Leon Loveridge — studying relational formulations of quantum systems, symmetry, and the role of correlations in defining physical observables.
